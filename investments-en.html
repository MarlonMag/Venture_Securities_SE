Some of our previous investments

(year of first investment and exit respectively)

Socket Communications, Inc. (1996-2000)

In 1994, we invested in Cetronic AB and a couple of years later worked for a merger with Socket Communications in Silicon Valley. However, Socket's cash was depleted and we invested in several rounds, becoming the second-largest shareholder. The business developed slowly and the merger was cancelled, but suddenly the company's products for digital communication became in demand. We listed the company on NASDAQ and the shares became 1999's winner. At our first investment, the company value was $4 million and this increased to a peak of $1.2 billion! We started selling shares in 1999 and sold the last ones a couple of years later with a very good profit.

socketmobile.com →

NeoPharma AB (1998-2004)

Together with our partners at FöretagsByggarna, we were the first investors in the Uppsala company NeoPharma. The company developed a new distribution method for medications for patients with severe Parkinson's disease. In 2004, NeoPharma was acquired by Belgian Solvay for over 600 million SEK.

ProwebCE S.A. (2000-2015)

We were the first investor in this French start-up that wanted to become a factory outlet on the internet. However, the market was not mature, so after three years of near-death experiences, the company focused on software for employee benefits and eventually also various digital offers. In 2010, the company was listed on NYSE Euronext and a few years later we took it private again. In 2017, the company was acquired by the French group Edenred at a valuation equivalent to over 3 billion SEK.

prowebce.com →

Nordisk Väntrums-TV AB (2011-2017)

In 2010, we participated in a guarantee consortium for Procast Media AB listed on Aktietorget. The issue failed and we were left with a large shareholding. It soon became apparent that the business was based on pure fraud and the company went bankrupt. (The CEO and CFO were later convicted of fraud and accounting violations.) However, we believed in the basic idea of TV screens at dentists and healthcare centers and therefore bought out the bankruptcy estate and formed Nordisk Väntrums-TV. After a couple of years, we merged with our worst competitor and since then the business has been profitable. In 2017, the company was contributed to Add Health Media AB, now Curus, in which we have been shareholders since then (see "Current Holdings" above).

nvtv.se →
