About us

Venture Securities is a private investment company for investments in unlisted companies. We started investing in startups as early as 1984 and have so far been active partners in about 80 companies.

HOW IT ALL BEGAN...

1984

Lars Lindgren forms Venture News AB which publishes monthly newsletters about developments in the venture capital market in Sweden and internationally.

1985

Lars Lindgren forms the Swedish Private Equity & Venture Capital Association (SVCA) and works as executive member 1985-91 and 1993-95 as well as chairman 1996-97.

1986

The company changes its name to Venture Securities and focuses on new issues to unlisted companies together with Stockholm Fondkommission.

1988

Venture Securities forms Campanius Venture KB, Europe's first fund-of-funds for existing shares in international VC funds. Campanius becomes a partner in funds mainly in the USA but also in the UK and France.

1988-90

Venture Securities carries out capital raising for several unlisted companies and builds up a rather scattered portfolio with own holdings in various imaginative projects around the world, many of which go to waste.

Based on the lessons learned during the 1980s, we now focus on investments in Swedish start-ups in industries where we feel comfortable, where we can work closely with the founders and where we have the opportunity to contribute to positive development. This changed focus has so far been very successful.

THE BUSINESS ANGEL SYNDICATE FÖRETAGSBYGGARNA

In 1993, FöretagsByggarna AB was formed where Lars Lindgren invested together with Martin Gemvik, Sture Lundén, Per-Henrik Norhagen and Johan Edfeldt, among others.

FöretagsByggarna made many very successful investments, including NeoPharma which developed medication for Parkinson's patients and was sold to Solvay in 1999 and the scheduling company Time Care which was sold to Allocate Software in 2001.

During the years 2003-06, FöretagsByggarna managed the VC funds Slottsbacken I and II with holdings in Sweden, Norway, Finland and the USA. One of the investments that the owners thought should be shut down was Marratech in Luleå, which we managed to sell to Google and the company's technology later became part of Google Hangout.

FORMATION OF DHS VENTURE PARTNERS

The CEOs of DHS Venture Partners' first four investments. From left Andreas Willgert from Formulate, Alexander Hjertström from Airinum, Hjalmar Ståhlberg Nordegren from Karma and Ludvig Persson Lejon from Qasa

Already 20 years ago, Lars Lindgren began thinking about starting a business angel network with alumni from the Stockholm School of Economics who would invest in startups with founders who had attended SSE. The motive was to help talented entrepreneurs and to "give back" to the school, but of course also to have fun together with other SSE alumni.

In 2014, he took the initiative to form DHS Venture Partners. DHS consists of 25 partners who all attended SSE and who invest their own money in parallel with DHS. We have so far invested in just over thirty startups. The very first investments were in Karma and then Qasa, Airinum, Formulate, Insurello and several other exciting companies. The value of the DHS portfolio has more than tripled since the start in 2015, so it's going in the right direction!

www.dhsventurepartners.com

PARTICIPATION IN THE PROPEL FUNDS

In 2014, STING (Stockholm Innovation & Growth) formed the first Propel fund to invest in the 16 companies that were accepted into STING's accelerator program. The money for Propel came from 20 selected business angels and among the companies the fund invested in can be mentioned Apprl, Sellpy, Sudio and Qasa.

The following year, Propel II was formed, which invested in Airinum, Beatly, Impactpool, Match2One and World's Sports Group, among others.

Propel III was formed in 2016 and has invested in 26 companies, Propel IV was formed in 2017 and has invested in Learnster among others, Propel V was formed in 2019, Propel VI was formed in 2022 and Propel VII was formed in 2025.

We have invested in all Propel funds and have also made active investments directly in several of the companies (see under the "Investments" tab). Lars Lindgren is a board member of the latest Propel funds.

Since 2019, Venture Securities is one of a few selected investors who have a so-called co-investment agreement with the state-owned Saminvest.

INVESTMENTS IN VENTURE CAPITAL FUNDS

To broaden the network and learn more about different industries, Venture Securities has invested in a few additional funds:

Streamlined Ventures (2013)

In 2013, we became the only European limited partner in Streamlined Ventures based in Palo Alto. Streamlined has invested in about 40 American start-ups and participates in seed and A rounds.

Streamlined's first investment was in Applovin which at the IPO in 2021 received a valuation of nearly 400 times the invested capital and now has a market value of over USD 100 billion.

Another of Streamlined's first investments was in DoorDash which went public in December 2020 at a valuation of over 1,000 times the invested capital.

streamlinedventures.com

GP Bullhound Sidecar III (2014)

In 2014, we invested in GP Bullhound Sidecar III which in turn has invested in Spotify, Klarna, Matsmart and LeoVegas, among others.

gpbullhound.com

SSE Ventures (2022)

In 2022, we joined SSE Ventures which invests in startups with connections to the Stockholm School of Economics. We are also part of the admissions committee that selects which companies get to participate in SSE Business Lab.

SSE Business Lab

RECOGNITION AND AWARDS

Lars Lindgren's long-standing commitment as a business angel has been recognized within the industry. In 2024, he was awarded Nordic Angels' prestigious award "Most Active Angel 2024" at the Angel Prize gala.

This award recognizes his extensive investment activity and active participation in the Swedish startup ecosystem over four decades.
