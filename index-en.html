Our investment philosophy: F.U.N.

When we invest, we base our decisions on our three guiding principles F.U.N. - a philosophy that has guided us for over 40 years.

F

Fun

It's important that we find it enjoyable to work together. This applies to both the entrepreneurs and the various co-investors.

U

Useful

We want to be able to learn something interesting from the various businesses, and our experience from previous investments should also be useful for the companies.

N

Necessary

We aim for 10 times the money when selling a holding. We often remain as shareholders until the companies are acquired, which can take 5-10 years. (In one case it took 15 years but it became a very good investment in the end!)

Things we like in start-ups

The following points are not absolute requirements, but they dramatically increase the likelihood that we will want to invest:

The company sells a digital service, preferably with a SaaS model that provides recurring revenue

The company has some customers using the service

The company was started by at least a couple of founders

The founders have experience in entrepreneurship, understand technology, are highly driven and like to sell

The founders realize that when taking external capital, the company's best interests are most important, not the individual persons

The company needs at most 5-10 MSEK in a first external round and this money is enough to take the company to the next level

The company is valued at most 25-30 MSEK pre-money

Other skilled investors want to invest together with us

The company is based in Stockholm (for practical reasons since we like to be close to the entrepreneurs)

Things we can contribute

After over 80 investments in startups since 1984, we have accumulated quite a bit of experience that we are happy to share:

Our sweet spot is developing companies from 0-100 MSEK

We have experience from active board work in around 40 companies across different industries and development phases

Many companies have near-death experiences and it's good to have investors who have been through this before

After over 45 years in business, we have a very broad network of contacts that can be of great benefit

We organize meetings for the CEOs in "our" companies where they can exchange experiences in informal settings

We also organize workshops in selected areas

We invest up to 5 MSEK per company (sometimes more)

We prefer to start with a smaller investment and then increase as the company develops

We usually remain as shareholders until the company is acquired

My Life as a Business Angel

Lars Lindgren started as a business angel as early as 1984 after having had his own company, been a bank employee, worked at Aktiefrämjandet and been information manager at the listed development company Hexagon. The year after, he took the initiative to form the Swedish Private Equity & Venture Capital Association (SVCA) and has since been very active in developing the ecosystem around venture capital in Sweden.

In this book, he shares his personal experiences from investments in a total of 80 startups. Some have gone well, some have gone badly and some bad ones have been turned into something good. The book contains detailed stories about a dozen of these investments – both good and bad – and the lessons he has learned from them.

Business angels play a very important role in identifying and financing startups that can eventually grow up and become successful companies. But can anyone become a business angel and how do you get started? How do you find startups and what should you think about when investing? What do you do if the company doesn't develop as intended? And is it really possible to make a living being a business angel? All these questions and many more are answered in this book!

"My Life as a Business Angel" is available for purchase at Akademibokhandeln on Mäster Samuelsgatan and at Hedengrens in Sturegallerian in Stockholm as well as at Adlibris and Bokus or directly from the publisher.
